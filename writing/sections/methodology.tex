\section{Methodology}
Attempting to do a Hawaii-specific analysis is quite difficult given how small the islands are and how sparse the data can be. 
Despite this, we make do with what is available.

\subsection*{Data Sources}
To do this analysis, we primarily use the American Community Survey prepared by the Census Bureau. 
Here, we leverage the geographical hierarchy that the Census provides, where we specifically look at the Tract level. 

Additionally, we also leverage IRS Statistics of Income (SOI) data in order to gain a better picture of the taxes and charitable contributions that the wealthiest makes.

\subsection*{How do we measure the wealthy?}

It is quite difficult to measure wealthy people due to many public sources of data needing to be anonymized, 
with IRS SOI data having the highest brackets for adjusted gross income just be over \$200,000.
